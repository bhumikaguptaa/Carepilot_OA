Question: what is botulism?
AI Response: Botulism is a rare but serious paralytic illness caused by a toxin produced by *Clostridium botulinum* bacteria. The toxin attacks the body's nerves, causing muscle weakness, blurred or double vision, drooping eyelids, slurred speech, difficulty swallowing, and eventually paralysis that can lead to respiratory failure. It is typically acquired through contaminated food (foodborne botulism), contaminated wounds (wound botulism), or when infants ingest spores that grow in their intestines (infant botulism). Botulism is a medical emergency requiring prompt treatment.
